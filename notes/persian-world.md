# People profile

The Persian world consists of peoples who are ethnically, linguistically, and historically
related to the Persian or Iranian people. It is a vast region in Asia, extending from
Azerbaijan and Iran in the West through Afghanistan (where 80% speak Dari, a Persian
dialect), Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan in the northeast. Iran is the heartland of the
Persian world, where an estimated 65-79 percent are Persian.

Persians were never Arabized. Persians are justly proud of their long and ancient history.
They have retained their language and rich culture for thousands of years. Many in the
Persian world live in challenging conditions and have limited political or religious
freedom.

Many say that Persian people are more responsive to the Gospel today than any other ethnic
group in the world.

However, in some countries in the region, there are almost no known believers nor any
movements to Christ.
