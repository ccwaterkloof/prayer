# Strategic prayer

- Praise God for relative stability in Burundi after the violent terrors of the 1990s and
  of 2015. An elected government now includes both major ethnic groups (Hutu and Tutsi).
  Pray for the new parliament elected in 2025. Pray for genuine respect and cooperation
  between Hutu and Tutsi as these two peoples build Burundi’s future together.
- Corruption remains a challenge in the country in general, as does the tight grip of
  poverty and regional instability. Pray for change that lasts, which only the gospel can
  bring.
- Praise for sustained evangelical growth, although this now has slowed compared to the
  tumultuous 1990s. Nearly all Protestant and Anglican churches have seen significant
  growth, even during the periods of war.
- 45% of the population is under 15 years old
- There is a genuine need for expatriate Christian workers. Between 1970 and 1985, nearly
  all missionaries were expelled by the regime of that time; few returned thereafter due
  to ongoing instability. Many are needed to work with national leaders in areas of
  discipleship, theological education, reconciliation and trauma counselling, literacy and
  education and holistic development.
